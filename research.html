Research Projects

Active federally supported work in waste-to-energy gasification, clean hydrogen generation, digital engineering, and community-centered energy technology adoption.

DOE Project $750,000 Fluidized bed gasification

Overcoming Technical and Community Barriers to Adopting Gasification Technologies

This project demonstrates a 300 kWth pilot-scale pressurized MSW-biomass fluidized-bed co-gasifier while studying the operating conditions, feedstock parameters, and communication methods needed for adoption of sustainable gasification technologies.

MSW + Biomass Hydrogen-rich syngas TEA/LCA HDSTEM outreach

Technical scope

System configuration analysis, gasifier design, construction, testing, feedstock characterization, and process sensitivity studies for hydrogen production and syngas quality.

Community scope

Communication infrastructure and persuasive messaging to educate policymakers, stakeholders, and the public about co-gasification benefits.

DOE Project $1.5M Digital Engineering

Digital Engineering Coalition for Energy Systems Innovation

This coalition develops a digital engineering framework for energy systems, using the Center’s biomass gasifier as a training and demonstration platform for next-generation energy engineers.

Digital twin Digital thread Gasifier demonstration Workforce training

Digital workflow

Links simulation, CAD, MBSE, IoT, stakeholder interfaces, AI/ML, cloud tools, TEA/LCA, and physical assets through a connected digital thread.

Project partners

Built around a university-led coalition involving UTEP, the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley, and Angelo State University.
